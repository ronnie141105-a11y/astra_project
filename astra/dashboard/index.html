Onset · Peak · Dissipation Timeline

ASTRA is advisory-only: it never issues a clearance. Once an FMP accepts a dissipation candidate here, the following steps happen outside this tool, by phone/radio, per current ATFCM practice:

  1. FMP-E coordinates the accepted solution with the ACC supervisor (SUP-E).
  2. FMP-E coordinates with FMP-A at the affected upstream ACC(s).
  3. FMP-A briefs the planner controller (PLC-A) of the affected sector.
  4. PLC-A instructs the executive controller (EC-A), who issues the clearance to the pilot.
